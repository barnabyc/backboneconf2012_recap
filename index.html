BackBoneConf 2012

Lets do the recap!

2 day conference in Boston

12 speakers

~300 attendees.

What is BackBone?

It's a JS library!

A very, very small library that provides basic MVC capabilities and encourages modular application design.

github.com/documentcloud/backbone

1. Keynote

✭✭✭✭✩

  • Emphasized: Separate your concerns. Don't do JS the old way. Get your state out of the DOM! Use Models to actually Model your data! It really is possible to separate your concerns in a browser! ZOMG!
  • Detail: BackBone came from DocumentCloud. All open source and highly accessible thanks to minimal size.
  • Popularity: Quite. Lots of your favorite sites built with BackBone: Pandora, Rdio, Trello, MetaLab Flow, FourSquare, Khan Academy!
  • Where are we? We already hold these principles dear. We know this is the future. We're ahead of a lot of the audience.
speakerdeck.com/u/jashkenas/p/state-of-the-backbone

2. Ember.js

✭✭✭✩✩

  • Emphasized: Ember is better than BackBone. (Enemy territory much?)
  • Detail: BackBone is too small and you really need a more comprehensive framework to do anything substantial.
  • Popularity: Unclear. Some of the features of Ember are appealing, but in terms of keeping a project slim, are likely overkill.
  • Where are we? We looked at Ember's early incarnations, and have kept an eye out, but nothing compelling to make us reconsider our choices.
speakerdeck.com/u/wycats/p/backboneconf-emberjs

3. How to Argue about JavaScript

✭✭✭✩✩

  • Emphasized: Civility is key to collaborative endeavors.
  • Detail: JS is an evolving language, and not everybody agrees on how to write it. If you have strong opinions and little tact, you can easily end-up missing the point.
  • Popularity: It was a good wake-up call to a lot of the attendees as getting more involved in the community means learning how to speak to people.
  • Where are we? Most of the time we're all polite and constructive, but even we have our moments of bluntness...
slideshare.net/savetheclocktower/how-to-argue-about-javascript-13167253

4. JS Module Patterns

✭✭✭✩✩

  • Emphasized: Organize your application code for sanity and performance!
  • Detail: In addition to how to model your application, don't overlook your file structure. Split code into modules, load them with a require construct, deprecate during refactor.
  • Popularity: Somewhat. A lot of folks are really getting into the core concepts of MVC, but overall app structure is still secondary.
  • Where are we? Solid footing. Our JS code organization and structure has evolved and we're continuing to improve asset packaging and performance.
alexsexton.com/talks/backboneconf2012

5. Data Binding

✭✭✭✭✩

  • Emphasized: Seamless 2-way data binding is an ideal of user/data interaction.
  • Detail: Demonstrated very concisely how easy it is to abstract user model changes automatically reflected in views, and vice-versa (using his AngularJS framework).
  • Popularity: Minimal. It's a fairly advanced topic that is most easily accomplished with a framework that handles both the model and the view logic, so not as simply as just bolting on a templating engine to a BackBone app.
  • Where are we? Early stages. We've had this need before, and done a good job at implementing it, but nothing as seamless and transparent as AngularJS provides.
vojtajina.github.com/html5la/2012-05-30-boston

6. BackBone from a Dojo-er

✭✭✩✩✩

  • Emphasized: View handling in BackBone is a little lacking.
  • Detail: Dojo is another JS framework. Rebecca hasn't used BackBone much. She explained how she found herself writing some view handling code that she wouldn't have had to in Dojo.
  • Popularity: Unclear. Jeremy's take away (as noted by almost all speakers and participants) was to add some helper methods for view destroying and event unbinding.
  • Where are we? Solid footing. We started well and continue to improve our event management within long-lived models to short-lived view scenarios.
speakerdeck.com/u/rmurphey/p/new-dogs-old-tricks-or-dojo-already-did-that

7. Lumbar

✭✭✭✭✩

  • Emphasized: BackBone is slim on purpose, here's some extra modules and advice to help.
  • Detail: Additional modules plus a lot of suggested patterns to improve maintainability.
  • Popularity: Growing. A lot of the attendees were interested and asked detailed questions about models, data types, templating, etc.
  • Where are we? Ahead of the curve. We've taken good advantage of YUI modules and patterns, and written many abstractions ourselves. If we were using BackBone, undoubtedly Lumbar would be in our repo but we've still be using a lot of the same patterns.
speakerdeck.com/u/braddunbar/p/lumbar-support

8. Migrating a Large App to BackBone

✭✭✭✩✩

  • Emphasized: NewsBlur is much more maintainable and performant since migrating.
  • Detail: Long, winding examples highlighting very fragile linkages when truth is held in DOM elements. Ultimately proving that porting a large application is possible. (War stories always reassuring.)
  • Popularity: Strong. The attendees were all basically in the camp that MVC is better than DOM, but it was reassuring to hear.
  • Where are we? Ahead of the curve. We've already learned from the trials of complex, inter-dependent DOM spaghetti; bless our MVC saviors!
speakerdeck.com/u/samuelclay/p/backbonification-migrating-newsblur-from-dom-spaghetti-to-backbonejs

9. Real-World Realtime

✭✭✭✭✩

  • Emphasized: Robust app structure will make data updates inconsequential to manage.
  • Detail: There is no perfect real-time solution, but thanks to sound data modeling and a solid MVC approach, you shouldn't need to worry that your data may or may not be "real-time".
  • Popularity: Minimal. It's a somewhat niche requirement, and every application is different, but it was well-received to hear Henrik's war stories.
  • Where are we? Good understanding. We've tried some real-time (remember Pusher guys?) but haven't had the need to apply real-time data to our revent MVC JS.
speakerdeck.com/u/henrikjoreteg/p/real-world-realtime-with-backbone

10. Airbnbâ€™s Journey into Mobile Web

✭✭✭✭✭

  • Emphasized: War story about building AirBNB's mobile web experience from ground up with BackBone
  • Detail: Very short deadline, never used BackBone before, didn't really have a clear vision defined - still got it done in time.

12. JS and Real Languages

✭✭✭✭✩

  • Emphasized: Use TDD. Use documentation. Don't rely on Cucumber and acceptance tests because they're big and slow.

  • Popularity: Minimal. These really are areas without a great deal of sexy adoption right now, but they're evolving.
  • Where are we? Cutting edge. We are demanding of our TDD tools and rigorous about our code documentation.
